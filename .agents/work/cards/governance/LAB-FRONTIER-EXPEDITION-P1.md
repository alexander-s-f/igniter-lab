## Headline Findings

- **I1:** Igniter's identity (Epistemic Contract Language) competes on a 4th axis — *safety from
  dishonesty* — that no mainstream language occupies; expedition selection criterion = "does it
  deepen the honesty moat?"
- **I2 (sharpest):** the Accountability Loop is half-built — observe/estimate enforced, but
  **Decide (Gap-J) / Audit (Gap-N)** are spec_candidate only. *The language can be honest about
  what it knows but not yet about what it chooses.* Enforcement inversion: central postulates
  P22–P26 are the least built.
- **I3:** Igniter is ~70% of an **agent-era accountability substrate** (typed model observations +
  mandatory uncertainty + No-Upward-Coercion + our P4 `model→needs_human_review` VM proof); missing
  piece = human-review gate as a contract primitive.
- **I4:** "receipt = evidence, not authority" found in 5 domains → design-law candidate; combined
  with receipt determinism (LAB-CONCURRENCY-P3 re-execution parity) + program identity + TBackend
  ⇒ **deterministic replay** is a latent platform property.
- **I5:** shadow production (sparkcrm-shadow) = third verification mode; adoption as evidence
  collection ("99% shadow accuracy = the moment igniter becomes an alternative").
- **I6:** white space — `constraints{}`, `StrategyDecision.rejected`, `SimulationReceipt`,
  `PostAuditReceipt`, `History[T]`, convergence loops, cancellation, human-gate: **zero lab
  pressure**; meanwhile `fold_stream`/TBackend/forms are *ahead* of what canon admits.
- **I7:** unique-concept shortlist: choice honesty, audit debt, declarative ethical constraints,
  synthetic-world quarantine, reversibility algebra, bitemporal cache freshness.
